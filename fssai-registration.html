FSSAI Registration Overview

Every food business in India, whether a small vendor or a large manufacturer, must obtain FSSAI registration or license as mandated by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India. This ensures that the food sold is safe, hygienic, and fit for consumption.

FSSAI registration is based on the size and nature of your business and is categorized into three types: Basic Registration, State License, and Central License. The process requires documentation, compliance with hygiene standards, and regular updates. Timely registration helps maintain public trust and avoids penalties.

What is FSSAI Registration?

FSSAI Registration is a 14-digit unique number issued to food businesses that confirms compliance with food safety standards. This number must be printed on food packaging and displayed at business premises. It is mandatory for all entities involved in the food supply chain, including manufacturing, processing, packaging, storage, distribution, and retail.

Why is FSSAI Registration Important?

  • Legal Requirement: Operating without an FSSAI license is punishable under the FSS Act.
  • Builds Consumer Trust: Shows your commitment to food safety and hygiene.
  • Business Expansion: Essential for opening new outlets, online food delivery tie-ups, or exports.
  • Improves Credibility: Boosts brand image and provides assurance to stakeholders and customers.

Who Needs an FSSAI License?

  • Petty Food Businesses: Small vendors, home-based kitchens, food stalls – need Basic Registration.
  • Medium Enterprises: Restaurants, manufacturers, and traders – require State License.
  • Large Enterprises or Exporters: Big manufacturers, importers/exporters, and MNCs – require Central License.

Steps Involved in the FSSAI Registration Process:

  • Determine applicable license type (Basic, State, or Central)
  • Prepare documents like ID/address proof, business details, and safety plans
  • File the application through the FoSCoS portal
  • Track application and respond to any queries from the authority
  • Receive your FSSAI Registration Certificate upon approval

Key Points to Remember:

  • Registration is mandatory before starting any food-related business
  • License is typically valid for 1 to 5 years and must be renewed before expiry
  • All food packages must carry the 14-digit FSSAI license number
  • Failure to register can result in fines up to ₹5 lakhs or imprisonment

Eligibility Criteria for FSSAI Registration

Understand which category of FSSAI registration or license your food business falls under:

  • Basic Registration Required for petty food businesses with an annual turnover of up to ₹12 lakhs. This includes home kitchens, street vendors, hawkers, and small-scale food retailers.
  • State License For businesses with turnover between ₹12 lakhs and ₹20 crores operating within one state. Applicable to restaurants, mid-sized manufacturers, storage units, and distributors.
  • Central License Mandatory for large-scale businesses with turnover above ₹20 crores or those engaged in food imports, exports, or operations across multiple states.
  • Transporters & Distributors Entities involved in transporting, storing, or distributing food must register or get licensed based on their operational scale and region.

FSSAI License Renewal Process

FSSAI licenses are valid for 1 to 5 years and must be renewed before expiry to avoid penalties or cancellation. Renewal should ideally be initiated at least 30 days before the license expires.

Steps to Renew Your FSSAI License:

  1. Check Validity: Confirm the expiry date of your existing license and ensure you’re eligible for renewal.
  2. Gather Documents: Collect required documents like ID proof, existing license copy, business registration proof, FSMS declaration, etc.
  3. Submit Online Application: Login to the FoSCoS portal and file your renewal application with updated details.
  4. Pay Government Fees: Make the applicable fee payment based on license type and validity period.
  5. Await Approval: FSSAI officials may verify documents or conduct an inspection before approving the renewal.
  6. Receive Renewed License: Once approved, download the renewed certificate from the portal and continue operations without interruption.

Note: Late renewal may attract a penalty of ₹100 per day. If your license expires, you may need to apply for a fresh license.

FSSAI Registration FAQs

What is FSSAI registration?

FSSAI registration is a mandatory license issued by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India to regulate the quality and safety of food products sold in India. It is required for all food-related businesses.

Who needs to apply for FSSAI registration or license?

Any individual or business involved in the manufacturing, processing, packaging, storage, distribution, or sale of food must obtain an FSSAI registration or license based on turnover and operational size.

What are the types of FSSAI licenses?

There are three types: Basic Registration (for turnover up to ₹12 lakhs), State License (₹12 lakhs to ₹20 crores), and Central License (above ₹20 crores or inter-state/import-export businesses).

What documents are required for FSSAI registration?

Documents include identity/address proof, business registration proof, FSMS declaration, passport-size photo, and other supporting documents depending on the license type.

How long does it take to get FSSAI registration?

It typically takes 3–7 working days for Basic Registration and up to 30 days for State or Central License, depending on the type of business and response from the authority.

Is FSSAI license mandatory for home-based food businesses?

Yes, even home-based food sellers and cloud kitchens must obtain FSSAI Basic Registration if their turnover is under ₹12 lakhs per annum.

Can I operate in multiple states with one FSSAI license?

No, for operations across multiple states, a Central License is required for the head office, and separate State Licenses are needed for each operational unit.

How long is the FSSAI license valid?

FSSAI licenses are valid for 1 to 5 years, depending on what you choose at the time of application. Renewal should be done before expiry to avoid penalties.

Can I renew or modify my FSSAI license?

Yes, licenses can be renewed before expiry and modified in case of any changes in business details, such as address, contact info, or food category.

What are the penalties for not having an FSSAI license?

Operating without a valid FSSAI license can result in a penalty of up to ₹5 lakhs and even imprisonment under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006.
